Overview

What It Is

A VR experience built in Unity for Meta Quest that places users behind the wheel of a car and challenges them to navigate traffic while facing realistic distractions. Designed to make the dangers of distracted driving feel real rather than theoretical.

The Goal

To support driver education programs by giving young drivers an immersive, consequence-free way to experience what distracted driving actually feels like — before they encounter it on the road.

My Role

I've been responsible for core systems programming — building out the VR vehicle controls, the tutorial sequence, cinematic camera logic, and custom shader effects.

Key Features

The systems I built and what makes each one interesting technically.

FEATURE 01

VR Car with Driving Functionality

A fully functional VR vehicle built from the ground up for Meta Quest. The car responds to the player's physical inputs in VR — steering, throttle, and braking — with vehicle physics tuned to feel realistic without causing simulator sickness.

XR Toolkit Vehicle Physics Meta Quest
FEATURE 02

Tutorial Sequence

A guided onboarding sequence that walks new users through the VR controls before putting them on the road. The tutorial is paced using coroutines and trigger zones, keeping the player moving through steps naturally without needing a menu or UI to advance.

C# Coroutines Trigger Zones UX Design
FEATURE 03

Cinematic Sequence

A scripted cinematic moment that plays at a key story beat in the experience. Built by repositioning the XR Origin to move the camera through a pre-authored path, with a vignette fade via URP Post Processing to transition in and out cleanly without breaking VR immersion.

XR Origin Post Processing URP Vignette Cinemachine
FEATURE 04

Fire Shader Effect

A custom fire shader built in Unity's Shader Graph for URP, used to sell the aftermath of a collision in the experience. The effect uses scrolling noise textures and vertex displacement to create a convincing flame without the performance cost of a particle-heavy solution — important for hitting frame rate targets on Quest.

Shader Graph URP Noise Textures Vertex Displacement
FEATURE 05

Sound Manager

A centralized audio management system that handles sound playback across the experience. Built as a singleton so any script can trigger sounds without needing a direct reference, keeping audio logic decoupled from gameplay systems and making it easy to add new sounds as the project grows.
